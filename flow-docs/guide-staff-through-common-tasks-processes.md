<p>Defining and enforcing consistent business processes is one of the main reasons people use model-driven apps. Consistent processes help make sure people using the system can focus on their work and not on remembering to perform a set of manual steps. Processes can be simple or complex and can change over time.</p>
<p>PowerApps includes several  types of processes, each designed for a different purpose:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Business process flows</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Mobile task flows</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Workflows</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Actions</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Similar to processes, you can also create  business rules and recommendations. For more information, see <a href="/MicrosoftDocs/powerapps-docs-pr/blob/release-crm-migration/powerapps-docs/maker/model-driven-apps/create-business-rules-recommendations-apply-logic-form.md">Create business rules and recommendations to apply logic in a form</a></p>
<blockquote>
<p>[!NOTE]
Using processes can impact the license requirements for PowerApps and flows. For more information, see <a href="https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/powerapps/maker/common-data-service/data-platform-entity-licenses" rel="nofollow">Entity license requirements</a>.</p>
</blockquote>
<p><a name="user-content-BKMK_BP"></a></p>
<h2><a id="user-content-when-to-use-business-process-flows" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#when-to-use-business-process-flows"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>When to use business process flows</h2>
<p>Use a business process flow when you want staff to move through the same stages and follow the same steps to interact with a customer. For example, use a business process flow if you want everyone to handle customer service requests the same way, or to require staff to gain approval for an invoice before submitting an order.</p>
<p>Your environment includes several ready-to-use business process flows for common sales, service, and marketing tasks that you can use with little or no changes required. Or, you can create your own. See the following topic for more information on business process flows:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/MicrosoftDocs/powerapps-docs-pr/blob/release-crm-migration/powerapps-docs/maker/model-driven-apps/create-business-process-flow.md">Create a business process flow</a></li>
</ul>
<p><a name="user-content-BKMK_TF"></a></p>
<h2><a id="user-content-when-to-use-mobile-task-flows" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#when-to-use-mobile-task-flows"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>When to use mobile task flows</h2>
<p>You can also design a type of business process flow called a task flow in Dynamics 365 for phones or Dynamics 365 for tablets based on common tasks your users perform. For example, if they need to regularly perform a series of follow-up steps after client meetings, create a task flow. When users tap the new task in their mobile app, it will lead them through from start to finish so they don't forget an important step. For more information on task flows, see the following topics:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="/MicrosoftDocs/powerapps-docs-pr/blob/release-crm-migration/powerapps-docs/maker/common-data-service/create-mobile-task-flow.md">Create a mobile task flow</a></li>
</ul>
<p><a name="user-content-BKMK_WF"></a></p>
<h2><a id="user-content-when-to-use-workflows" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#when-to-use-workflows"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>When to use workflows</h2>
<p>Use workflows to automate business processes behind the scenes. Workflows are typically initiated by system events so the user doesn't need to be aware that they are running. Workflows that operate in the background are "asynchronous." Workflows can also be configured for people to manually initiate them. when you want to automate common tasks, such as automatically sending a confirmation email to a customer when an order ships. Workflows that operate in real time are "synchronous." For more information on workflows, see  <a href="/MicrosoftDocs/powerapps-docs-pr/blob/release-crm-migration/powerapps-docs/maker/common-data-service/workflow-processes.md">Workflow processes</a></p>
<p><a name="user-content-BKMK_Actions"></a></p>
<h2><a id="user-content-when-to-use-actions" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#when-to-use-actions"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>When to use Actions</h2>
<p>Use Actions when you want to automate a series of commands in the system. Actions expand the vocabulary available for developers to express business processes. With core verbs like Create, Update, Delete, and Assign provided by the system, a Action uses those core verbs to create more expressive verbs like Approve, Escalate, Route, or Schedule. If the definition of a business process changes, someone who isn’t a developer can edit the Action so the code doesn’t need to be changed.  For more information on Actions, see  <a href="/MicrosoftDocs/powerapps-docs-pr/blob/release-crm-migration/powerapps-docs/maker/common-data-service/actions.md">Actions</a></p>
<p><a name="user-content-useMSFlow"></a></p>
<h2><a id="user-content-when-to-use-microsoft-flow" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#when-to-use-microsoft-flow"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>When to use Microsoft Flow</h2>
<p>Use Microsoft Flow when you need to create automated workflows that perform actions between your environment and favorite app or service, such as Dynamics 365, Twitter, Dropbox, Google services, Office 365, and SharePoint. You can trigger a flow based on a specific action, or invoke from within your app. More information: <a href="https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/dynamics365/customer-engagement/basics/use-flow-automate-processes-across-services" rel="nofollow">Use Microsoft Flow to automate processes across services</a></p>
<p><a name="user-content-BKMK_Where"></a></p>
<h2><a id="user-content-where-do-i-go-to-create-processes" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#where-do-i-go-to-create-processes"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Where do I go to create processes?</h2>
<p>There are two paths to navigate to processes:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Open <a href="/MicrosoftDocs/powerapps-docs-pr/blob/release-crm-migration/powerapps-docs/maker/model-driven-apps/advanced-navigation.md#solution-explorer">solution explorer</a> and go to <strong>Components &gt; Processes.</strong> This path provides convenient access when you are doing other customization work in the customization tools.</p>
</li>

</li>
</ul>
<p>Individual business process flows can also be edited using the <strong>Edit Process</strong> button in the command bar for the form where the business process flow is active.</p>
